The thesis

Language is linear because time is. Ideas are not — they branch, fork, and recombine. So a faithful map of reasoning needs at least two dimensions.

We advance fastest where reality slaps down errors: technology, mathematics, empirical science. But the questions people care about most — ethical, political, social — are often untestable: we cannot falsify our way to the answer. There, the honest best is not to declare a winner but to map the disagreement faithfully — every position's strongest form, its grounds and consequences, and exactly where reasonable people fork.

Reasonable decomposes a disputed question into atomic, truth-apt claims on a 2-D graph, connected by a small set of typed relations, and then assesses them with blind, reputation-weighted rating designed so a map's verdicts can approach truth without collapsing into a popularity vote. The aim is to raise the cultural floor for what counts as a reasonable argument — to make a weak belief as costly to hold in public as a checkable false fact became after Wikipedia.

What we are submitting

The competition invites a spec, a prototype tool, or a protocol. This is all three. Its center of gravity is the structure and assessment layers, but it reaches ingestion too: the AI build harnesses turn a raw source pack into an attributed graph today (claims extracted, provenance attached, duplicates merged), and the same open, append-only format is built to keep ingesting — a living graph any contributor, human or AI, adds to over time. Carried inside as evidence of rigor: a comparative methodology study and several critiques-with-counterexamples.

Prototype

A runnable engine

One stdlib-only Python interface (graph.py) over a layered engine: an append-only event log → deterministic fold → frozen snapshot, with the assessment stack (calibration, reputation, camp-detection, the contested/ghost lifecycle) live in the running code. No installs, no servers, no network.

Spec

Frozen contracts

The data-model and assessment contracts are written down with every key decision and tradeoff named — BUILD-SPEC, ASSESSMENT-SPEC, and the Evidence/Argument/Ought grammar spec. A judge can tell why it is shaped this way.

Protocol

Compounding by construction

The append-only graph format is interoperable: another team can extend a graph, reuse its anchors, and rebuild it byte-for-byte. Nothing is deleted — refuted material is demoted to a retrievable "ghost," never erased.

Viewer

The claim made visible

One self-contained HTML file (no deps, CSP-safe) renders all four case graphs and teaches its own visual grammar with a 90-second built-in Introduction — a non-technical reader needs no legend.

Run it, don't just read it. Everything reproduces from committed data with stdlib Python 3. From v0/: 
# the assessed verdict on COVID origins (contested)
python3 graph.py assess --data covid-graph-v2

# what's settled vs genuinely contested, and why
python3 graph.py contested --data debate-graph-v2

# determinism: same log + config → byte-identical snapshot
python3 graph.py rebuild --data blackholes-graph-v2
273 stdlib tests (incl. a determinism suite) pass from a clean checkout.

The tool, applied to whether the tool works

The FLF prompt itself cites Scott Alexander's "Your Attempt To Solve Debate Will Not Work" — the essay whose thesis is that projects exactly like this one are doomed. So we built the Reasonable graph of that debate: a faithful steelman of Scott's case, the project's counter-position, and honest concessions (three citing our own retired findings), then blind-panel-assessed it. Reconciled against Scott's actual post.

The assessed map did exactly what the design predicts — which is the whole point:

  • It refused to crown a winner and converged the diverse panel on the reframing. Scott's "mapping won't improve debate" (2.66) and our "a faithful map adds value" (2.93) both land genuinely contested; the highest-rated of the three top positions is the dissolution — "it depends what you demand of it" (3.91).
  • The deepest disagreement isolated itself as a value-crux. The two Ought nodes — "a faithful map is worth building even if it never changes minds" (endorse 3.84) vs "only worth it if it changes minds" (1.95) — are rated on democratic endorsement, never truth-weighted. The skeptic's dissent is preserved, not steamrolled.
  • It settled where the facts are settled — the evidence and the honest concessions are the lowest-dispersion nodes.

Scott's deepest point is that values, not facts, generate the disagreement, and no map resolves them. The grammar's answer is to put that very value-generator on the board as an explicit Ought crux, rated democratically — located but not resolved. The graph enacts the project's own thesis, on the debate about itself. (Full artifact: v0/debate-graph/REPORT.md; assessed set v0/debate-graph-v2/: 15 contested / 30 settled.)

Meaningfully better than off-the-shelf deep research

The judges' stated bar is "meaningfully better than off-the-shelf deep research on the same sub-question." We ran that head-to-head. Fed the same 24 sources / 80 claims a flat deep-research pipeline drew on — so representation is the only variable — the pipeline's adversarial verifier inverted the scientific consensus: it refuted six central peer-reviewed pro-zoonotic findings and confirmed contrarian preprints. The assessed graph recovered it.

COVID originsAssessed graphSignal
Natural zoonotic (market) origin3.34recovered as favored
Research-related (lab) incident2.17kept as a live minority
Market environmental samples (a refuted fact)4.3+re-affirmed as fact

The diagnosis is structural, not political: a binary adversarial filter (right for catching junk) misfires as a truth-weigher — default-to-refute fires on any confident claim in a contested field, and binary votes discard the fact inside a fact-plus-inference bundle. We then fixed it with a graded-verify step (separate provenance from a 0–5 merit score, drop default-to-refute), validated live. (v0/covid-graph/COMPARISON.md; v0/blackholes-graph/harness/workflow_deepresearch.js.)

The assessment spine — comparative & adversarial

The core question: in a collaborative map, how do you reliably find the truth-tracking raters and give their judgment due weight — so verdicts approach truth, cold-start from nothing, scale, survive genuine contestedness, and resist coordinated manipulation? We tested many methods against each other and followed the evidence, including where it refuted our own prior claims.

0.52→0.89
truth-tracking with consensus visible vs blind — the single biggest accuracy lever, enforced at the tool layer
60%

0.96–0.98

methods retired with counterexamples: alignment, raw discrimination on bias, superlinear weighting, BTS

The through-line

Every method that failed, failed the same way: a purely internal rule can only measure agreement with the crowd, so it inherits whatever the crowd gets systematically wrong. The fix is blind rating plus a small, frugal dose of external anchors, with reputation weight spent only where it is needed.

Counterexamples that force a re-think (dim 7)

  • Alignment buries the sharpest raters — rewarding proximity to the average is a tyranny of the median; every latent-truth variant inherits the majority's error.
  • Discrimination inverts on a biased crowd — it scores agreement with the biased consensus. Ship it only against an anchored consensus.
  • The sleeper defeats calibration — honest on the anchors, lying only in the un-anchored gaps; superlinear weighting makes it worse. Calibration's reach equals the anchors' coverage of the contested frontier.
  • MAE hides an over-certainty attack — a bloc pushing the already-leading answer can't flip the verdict, so it manufactures false certainty, collapsing a live question into fake near-settlement (the Semmelweis / minority-survival failure). Distance-to- oracle MAE is invisible to it; the verdict-certainty measure reveals it. The hardest adversarial case for a truth-finder is consensus entrenchment, not verdict-flipping — and it's the attack real agents most willingly serve.

Where we revised our own claims on new data (dim 5)

  • Calibration has an identifiability floor. Wired into a cooperative honest panel it degraded accuracy (held-out MAE 0.82 → 1.28) because clustered anchors give the affine fit no leverage. Fix, shipped: it now declines below an anchor-spread threshold and falls back to the raw aggregate byte-identically.
  • Dispersion is a reliable "contested" signal only when it's belief-camp-driven (between-group variance 82% for real camps vs 16% for good-faith panel noise). So the contested trigger is structural (rival-in-an-antithesis + spectral camp detection), not raw rating spread.

Structure catches what prose launders

A recurring result across every layer: the structured map mechanically surfaces defects that are invisible in the prose version of the same argument, and the project fixes them in the open.

  • Source-laundering becomes visible. In prose, "as Wikipedia notes…" reads identically to a primary citation. Every evidence node carries an explicit source, so a secondary aggregator posing as evidence can be audited and removed — as a reversible supersede, with a public reason, nothing deleted.
  • Is/ought category errors are impossible by construction. Hume's rule is enforced at the write boundary: an Ought may not ground a non-Ought. A whole class of smuggled value-premises simply cannot be written.
  • A single cheap judge is noise — for structure as much as for truth. A lone cheap "is this well-built?" probe swings ±13–28 points on identical reruns; the fix is the same panel-of-raters move that assessment uses for truth.

See v0/EVIDENCE-SOURCE-AUDIT.md and v0/PROJECT-OVERVIEW.md §3.

How it maps to the seven judging dimensions

  1. Epistemic uplift. Beats off-the-shelf deep research on the same COVID sub-question; makes load-bearing evidence and the fact/inference split visible; surfaces cruxes and value-vs-fact.
  2. Generalizability. One engine and workflow across all three case shapes plus the meta debate; nothing overfit to a single case.
  3. Compounding & shareability. Append-only, interoperable, rebuild-deterministic graph format; reusable anchors; per-region reputation.
  4. Scalability. Automated build/rate swarms; oracle spend scales with the number of contested divides, not users; a cheap/expensive tiering hybrid, honestly bounded.
  5. Methodological transparency. Every FINDINGS doc names its own limits; we revised our own prior claims on new data.
  6. Adversarial robustness. Survives a 60% biased majority in two domains; the sleeper and over-certainty attacks are named, bounded, and have built detectors.
  7. Insight contribution. A comparative methodology study plus counterexample-driven critiques and non-obvious tradeoffs (placement > quantity; disposition dominates capability; detection > correction).

The wider aim

These are three separate graphs only because we did not have time to build the single connected graph they belong to. Their topics already share deep underpinnings — biology, physics, mathematics — and the real target is one public graph of ~all knowledge, current and future.

Crucially, this need not stay a map of the already-known. A faithful map highlights its own holes, and can throw a target beyond the frontier: anyone could place a wanted node or neighborhood and put a bounty on developing it into high-confidence knowledge — much as this very competition does. Knowledge-building becomes a structured, incentivized, collaborative activity rather than a scatter of disconnected efforts.

The project has also opened a concrete near-term direction: running graphs with differing contributor sets — human and AI, which doubles as a new benchmark for AI models at direct knowledge-creation versus humans. The full concept (vision, mechanics, institution-assessment, a non-adversarial revenue model, and a machine-checkable-mathematics flagship) is in the Concept Overview and v0/SITE-VARIANTS-CONCEPT.md.

What is — and isn't — done

On the arguments themselves. The claims populating these graphs are AI-generated under rapid prototyping — chosen to make the format function and show its potential, not to be mature, fully-developed, or the definitive last word on eggs, COVID origins, or LHC safety. What we are demonstrating is the structure-and-assessment machinery; a graph worth taking as a settled verdict would be built and rated by far more contributors, over far more time, than a prototype allows.

The engine, the four assessed case graphs, and the assessment research are built and reproducible. Stated plainly, three further things are not yet done:

  • A human-rated slice. The ground-truth chain is model-generated except a thin external check — the highest-value gap, and the one that would break the model-on-model circularity.
  • An end-to-end longitudinal run of the full flywheel over many rounds with real churn — the components are validated, the integration is not yet exercised live.
  • The write-side of the viewer — collecting live blind ratings. The read-side renders the whole lifecycle today.

Overall confidence that the assembled machinery is robust for a real deployment: ~60–65% — high on the tested mechanisms, lower on transfer to the messy real thing. We would rather state that honestly than round it up.
