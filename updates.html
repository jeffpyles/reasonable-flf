2026-07-21 · 17:03 UTC

Vision doc: added "A translation layer between minds" to §6 — as AI reasoning outpaces human working memory, the graph renders machine-scale thinking into auditable, navigable, separately-assessable claims, so humans can still check work they could no longer follow as prose. Motivated by findings from a private research experiment comparing graph-structured and free-prose AI research records, where structured navigability proved to be the format's clearest advantage.

2026-07-20 · 21:44 UTC

Debate graph follow-up: re-seated the rebuttal that answered the just-strengthened skeptic claim. After the "2000 years unsolved" claim was steelmanned (it no longer asserts the problem is unsolvable), its old rebuttal was answering a point no longer being made. It was re-seated to engage the stronger claim directly — granting that the difficulty is real while arguing that past failure is weak evidence about this attempt, since the enabling conditions are new. Blind re-rated, it settled lower than before (3.4 vs 4.1) — the honest cost of rebutting a strong claim rather than a weak one. Details in v0/debate-graph-v2/README-STRUCTURE-PASS.md (Round 6.1).

2026-07-20 · 20:51 UTC

Debate graph: low-scoring supporting claims strengthened and re-rated. A review separated the debate graph's low-agreement claims into two kinds: those that are meant to score low (rival positions, a losing value-crux, honestly-weak skeptic claims — left alone) and a handful of supporting claims held down by fixable wording (overreach, bundling, or a strong point tied to a contested analogy). Those were reworked and re-rated blind by the same panel. A bundled three-part claim was split so its boldest part gets its own honest (lower) score; a skeptic claim was steelmanned from a fallacy into its strongest form (which the panel then rated highly, as it should). This was deliberately not an attempt to move the numbers our way — the aim is both sides expressed at full strength and judged on merit. Details in v0/debate-graph-v2/README-STRUCTURE-PASS.md (Round 6).

2026-07-20 · 19:21 UTC

Added a proposed grammar extension — a scenario / supposition construct — to the roadmap (under "Strengthen the arguments") and as a design note in v0/SPEC-evidence-argument-ought-ghosts.md §6.3. It lets the graph reason forward from a stipulated action ("if we did X, the world would be Y") and assess the consequences conditionally, exporting explicit "if X then Y" conditionals that can ground an ought — so the is/ought firewall stays intact while the system gains the forward model-building that real "what should we do?" questions turn on.
