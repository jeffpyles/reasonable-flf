# Covid graph — node dump for the oracle panel

Question: Did SARS-CoV-2 arise by natural zoonotic spillover or by a research-related incident?

Score EVERY node on Agreement (A): "on the merits, how well-supported / true is this claim?" 0.0 = clearly false/unsupported, 2.5 = genuinely uncertain or evenly balanced, 5.0 = clearly true/well-established. For an external-anchor (source-citing) node, judge FIDELITY + how well-established: does the cited source really support this, and how solid is it. For an interpretive claim, judge how strongly the evidence actually supports THAT interpretation — a genuinely contested crux should land near the middle, not the extremes.

## n001  [claim]  (source: -)
SARS-CoV-2 crossed into humans through a natural zoonotic spillover from an animal reservoir, most plausibly connected to wildlife trade at or near the Huanan Seafood Market, directly or via an intermediate host.

## n002  [claim]  (source: -)
SARS-CoV-2 entered the human population through a research-related incident connected to coronavirus research, such as work conducted at or associated with the Wuhan Institute of Virology, involving either a collected natural virus or a laboratory-modified one.

## n003  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-market-epicenter)
The earliest known COVID-19 cases and the SARS-CoV-2-positive environmental samples cluster geographically around the Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market in Wuhan, concentrated in the section of the market where live mammals were sold.

## n004  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-two-lineages)
Phylogenetic analysis of early SARS-CoV-2 genomes identifies two genetically distinct early lineages, designated A and B, both epidemiologically linked to the Huanan market.

## n005  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-raccoon-dna)
Genetic material from susceptible wildlife species, notably raccoon dogs, co-occurs with SARS-CoV-2-positive material in some Huanan market environmental samples; this establishes the animals' presence at the market, not confirmed infection or that they were the transmission source.

## n006  [claim]  (source: -)
The apparent geographic clustering of early COVID-19 cases and positive samples around the Huanan market is better explained by ascertainment and surveillance bias in early case-finding, which itself focused on the market, than by the market being the true site of a natural spillover.

## n007  [claim]  (source: -)
The clustering of early cases and environmental samples at the Huanan market reflects the true early epicenter of the outbreak, rather than being an artifact of where early surveillance happened to look.

## n008  [claim]  (source: -)
The existence of two genetically distinct early lineages, both linked to the Huanan market, indicates at least two independent zoonotic spillover events there, which best explains the pattern of early viral diversity.

## n009  [claim]  (source: -)
The inference that two early lineages prove multiple independent market spillovers rests on disputed phylogenetic and molecular-clock modeling assumptions, so a single introduction followed by early diversification, including via a research-related incident, cannot be excluded on this evidence alone.

## n010  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-defuse)
A 2018 DEFUSE proposal, submitted to DARPA by EcoHealth Alliance with Wuhan Institute of Virology collaborators, described plans including inserting furin cleavage sites into bat SARS-related coronaviruses; DARPA did not fund it.

## n011  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-ratg13-wiv)
RaTG13, the closest then-known relative of SARS-CoV-2 (~96% genome identity, still evolutionarily distant), was sampled and held at the Wuhan Institute of Virology, which conducted extensive bat-coronavirus research in the outbreak city.

## n012  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-no-intermediate)
As of the debate, no infected intermediate host animal or direct animal progenitor of SARS-CoV-2 had been identified, unlike SARS-CoV-1 (palm civets) or MERS (dromedary camels).

## n013  [claim]  (source: -)
The SARS-CoV-2 furin cleavage site is better explained by deliberate laboratory insertion than by natural acquisition, given documented WIV-collaborator plans (DEFUSE) to engineer exactly this kind of feature into bat coronaviruses shortly before the outbreak.

## n014  [claim]  (source: -)
DEFUSE's demonstrated intent and capability to engineer furin cleavage sites into bat coronaviruses, taken together with the Wuhan Institute of Virology's proximity to the outbreak and to the closest known relative (RaTG13), make a research-related incident the more likely explanation than treating each fact in isolation would suggest.

## n015  [claim]  (source: -)
DEFUSE, the WIV's research profile, and the furin cleavage site are independent circumstantial facts with no documented evidentiary link tying them into a single incident; treating them as a combined case mistakes coincidence of proximity for connection.

## n016  [claim]  (source: -)
The absence of any identified intermediate host or animal progenitor for SARS-CoV-2, unlike the civet (SARS-1) and camel (MERS) precedents, strongly favors a research-related origin over a natural spillover that has gone unusually unverified.

## n017  [claim]  (source: -)
The absence of an identified intermediate host is weak evidence given limited and late sampling, as with SARS-1's civet reservoir, which also took considerable time to establish and was aided by animals having already been sold or culled before targeted sampling began.

## n018  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-furin)
SARS-CoV-2 has a furin cleavage site (FCS) at the S1/S2 junction of its spike protein that is absent in its closest known sarbecovirus relatives and that enhances transmissibility; both camps accept that this feature exists and is functionally important.

## n019  [claim]  (source: -)
The furin cleavage site is better explained by natural acquisition than by deliberate laboratory insertion, since furin cleavage sites arise independently in numerous coronavirus lineages and the SARS-CoV-2 sequence shows no signature specific to laboratory manipulation rather than natural recombination or mutation.

## n020  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-proximal)
Andersen, Rambaut, Lipkin, Holmes and Garry's 'Proximal Origin' analysis (Nature Medicine, 2020) concluded that the SARS-CoV-2 genome shows no signature of deliberate genetic engineering, finding its receptor-binding domain and backbone more consistent with natural selection than with laboratory manipulation.

## n021  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-proximal)
The Proximal Origin authors privately expressed substantially more uncertainty about ruling out a lab-related origin, in contemporaneous messages later made public, than the published paper's confident conclusion conveyed.

## n022  [claim]  (source: -)
RaTG13's roughly 96% genome identity to SARS-CoV-2 reflects on the order of decades of evolutionary divergence between the two viruses, so RaTG13 itself cannot be a direct progenitor; WIV's possession of RaTG13 therefore establishes proximity to a distant relative, not access to the actual progenitor lineage, which limits how much weight the DEFUSE+WIV-proximity case can bear.

## n023  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-who-joint)
The WHO-convened China joint study assessed a zoonotic spillover pathway (direct or via an intermediate host) as likely-to-very-likely and rated a laboratory-related incident as extremely unlikely; the investigation's access to underlying data and sites was constrained by Chinese government control of the process, a limitation the WHO Director-General himself later noted publicly.

## n024  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-intel-assessments)
US intelligence agencies reached no consensus on SARS-CoV-2's origin: several assessed natural exposure as more likely, at least one assessed a laboratory-associated incident as more likely, and every agency reached its assessment with only low-to-moderate confidence.

## n025  [SOURCE-FACT]  (source: cov-debate)
A moderated public debate between a lab-leak proponent and a zoonosis proponent, judged by two independent adjudicators against a wagered stake, concluded in favor of natural zoonotic origin; as a single curated adjudication of one framing of the arguments, this result does not by itself settle the broader disagreement among expert institutions.

## n026  [claim]  (source: -)
Expert institutions assessing SARS-CoV-2's origin have not converged: the WHO-China joint study leaned toward zoonotic spillover as likely-to-very-likely while the US intelligence community split internally, with at least one agency favoring a lab-associated incident, and neither reached its conclusion with unconstrained access to the underlying evidence.

## n027  [claim]  (source: -)
Because institutional and adjudicated conclusions on SARS-CoV-2's origin diverge from one another and were each reached under significant access, scope, or framing constraints, they are best read as pointers to the reasoners' underlying evidence and its limits, not as terminal authorities that settle the question independent of that evidence.
