# Round 3 — converge and put judgment on record

Final round. Most items now have substantial ratings and, in places, real disagreement. Your job is to
leave the graph in the most reasonable state you can — using whatever standing your ratings have earned.

1. Re-read your persona brief (`eggs-p4/harness/personas/<your-id>.md`).
2. Read your round-3 assignment (`eggs-p4/harness/assign/r3/<your-id>.tsv`) and rate those items
   (read first; use the given `--bloc`).
3. **Close the loop on disagreement.** For assigned items and any you rated earlier, `list-comments`
   and see where things stand. Reply to arguments aimed at your view. Re-rate anything you now genuinely
   see differently after the full discussion — but only on the merits.
4. **Experts especially:** this is your last chance to move the graph. On the items where the crowd is
   clearly miscalibrated (weak inferences rated too high, fallacies rated as reasonable, well-scoped
   claims underrated), state your strongest concise case — a comment, a sharper phrasing, a corrective
   ground or antithesis — and record your rating. You are not here to conform; you are here to be right
   and to make being right legible.

Work item by item. When done, output one line:
`<your-id>: rated N, re-rated R, commented K, added M`.
